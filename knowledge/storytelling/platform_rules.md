# TheLifeCo Platform Rules & Content Guidelines

Comprehensive platform-specific rules, content specifications, and compliance guidelines for TheLifeCo wellness content.

---

## General Compliance Rules (All Platforms)

### Medical Claims Policy

**Never claim to cure, treat, or prevent any disease.** This applies across ALL platforms.

| Prohibited Language | Compliant Alternative |
|---------------------|----------------------|
| "Cure diabetes" | "Support blood sugar management" |
| "Treat anxiety" | "Support mental wellness" |
| "Prevent cancer" | "Support cellular health" |
| "Heal your gut" | "Support digestive wellness" |
| "Fix your hormones" | "Support hormonal balance" |
| "Reverse aging" | "Support cellular renewal" |

**Safe Phrases:**
- "May help support..."
- "Designed to promote..."
- "Can contribute to..."
- "Evidence suggests..."
- "Our guests report..."

### Testimonials & Results

- Always include disclaimer: "Individual results may vary"
- Never guarantee specific outcomes
- Before/after images require context and disclaimers
- Use specific timeframes when sharing results
- Avoid exaggerated claims even in testimonials

### Weight & Body Image

- Never use shaming language or "negative self-perception"
- Avoid before/after weight loss imagery on Meta platforms
- Focus on health markers, energy, and wellbeing over weight
- Don't promote dramatic body transformations
- Emphasize "vitality" and "energy" over "weight loss"

## Instagram

### Algorithm Priorities (2025)

Top 3 ranking factors:
1. **Watch Time** - How long people view your content
2. **Likes per Reach** - Engagement rate matters more than total likes
3. **DM Shares** - The strongest signal for reaching new audiences

### Feed Posts

**Specifications:**
- Image: 1080x1080px (square) or 1080x1350px (portrait - better performing)
- Caption: Up to 2,200 characters (first 125 visible before "more")
- Hashtags: 3-5 highly relevant (fewer is now better than 30)

**Best Practices:**
- Lead with faces - posts with people outperform everything else
- Front-load important information in first two lines
- Use line breaks for readability
- End with a question or CTA to boost comments

**Hashtag Strategy:**
- Mix of branded (#TheLifeCo), niche (#WellnessRetreat), and broad (#Health)
- Place in caption (not comments) for best reach
- Rotate hashtag sets to avoid appearing spammy

### Reels

**Specifications:**
- Length: 30-60 seconds optimal (can be up to 90 seconds)
- Aspect ratio: 9:16 (vertical)
- Hook: First 1-3 seconds critical

**Best Practices:**
- Use original audio or trending audio
- Add text overlays for muted viewing (85% watch without sound)
- Don't use TikTok watermarks (reduces distribution)
- Include face in first frame
- Avoid "salesy" content - educational/entertaining performs better

**Content Types That Perform:**
- Educational ("3 signs your body needs a detox")
- Behind-the-scenes at centers
- Guest transformation stories (with permission)
- Quick wellness tips
- "Day in the life" at TheLifeCo

### Stories

**Specifications:**
- 1080x1920px (9:16 ratio)
- Up to 60 seconds per story
- Multiple segments should total 3-7 stories

**Best Practices:**
- Post 3-5 quality stories throughout day (not 10+ at once)
- Use polls, quizzes, and questions for engagement
- Place important text in center (edges may be cut off)
- Add link stickers for CTAs
- Use countdown for program launches

### Carousels

**Specifications:**
- Up to 20 images/videos per carousel
- 1080x1350px recommended for all slides
- Can mix images and video clips

**Best Practices:**
- Educational carousels perform exceptionally well (10%+ engagement)
- Strong hook on first slide
- Each slide should provide value
- Last slide = strong CTA
- Use consistent visual theme

### Content Restrictions

Instagram limits distribution of:
- Health misinformation
- Before/after images implying dramatic weight changes
- Content promoting supplements without proper disclosures
- Eating disorder-related content (even recovery content can be flagged)
- Sexually suggestive content
- Third-party app watermarks (especially TikTok)
- Overly sales-focused content

## TikTok

### Platform Policies for Wellness Brands

**STRICTLY PROHIBITED:**
- Medical claims (treat, cure, prevent, diagnose)
- Weight loss product promotion
- Fat burning/appetite suppressant claims
- Detox product claims that imply medical benefits
- Body transformation promises
- Disordered eating content
- Fasting products marketed for weight loss

**TYPICALLY ALLOWED:**
- General wellness tips (exercise, sleep, balanced eating)
- Personal experience sharing (without transformation promises)
- Lifestyle content
- Educational content about health topics (factual, not promotional)
- Fitness content emphasizing long-term health (not weight loss)

### Branded Content Restrictions

TikTok's Branded Content Policy prohibits promoting:
- Medical institutions, treatments, or devices
- Nutritionists and telehealth providers
- Pharmacological products claiming health benefits
- Weight loss clinics and services
- Weight management supplements
- Fasting services and products

**Important**: This affects paid partnerships and influencer content. Organic content has more flexibility but must still comply with community guidelines.

### Content Specifications

**Videos:**
- Length: 15-60 seconds (can be up to 10 minutes)
- Aspect ratio: 9:16 (vertical only)
- Hook: First 1-2 seconds critical

**Best Practices:**
- Authenticity over polish - raw performs better
- Use trending sounds (but check for appropriateness)
- Text overlays essential
- Reply to comments with videos
- Post at least 1-3x daily for algorithm favor

### What Works for Wellness on TikTok

- "POV" style content
- "Things I learned at a wellness retreat"
- Recipe/food prep content
- Morning/evening routine content
- "What I eat in a day" (focus on nourishment, not restriction)
- Myth-busting wellness misinformation
- Behind-the-scenes authenticity

### What to Avoid

- Overly produced/corporate content
- Obvious advertisements
- Medical advice
- Weight loss promises
- "Transformation" content
- Clickbait that doesn't deliver

## YouTube

### Medical Misinformation Policy

YouTube removes content that contradicts health authority guidance in three categories:

1. **Prevention Misinformation**: False claims about preventing diseases/conditions
2. **Treatment Misinformation**: Promoting unproven treatments in place of medical care
3. **Denial Misinformation**: Denying existence of health conditions

**Specifically Prohibited:**
- Claims that alternative treatments replace medical care
- Anti-vaccine misinformation
- Content encouraging unproven remedies over professional treatment
- Claims that specific diets "cure" serious conditions

### Content Specifications

**Standard Videos:**
- Resolution: 1080p minimum, 4K preferred
- Aspect ratio: 16:9 (horizontal)
- Length: 8-15 minutes optimal for watch time
- Thumbnails: 1280x720px

**Shorts:**
- Length: Up to 60 seconds
- Aspect ratio: 9:16 (vertical)
- Hook: First 2-3 seconds critical

### Best Practices

**For Long-Form:**
- Use chapters/timestamps for organization
- Strong hook in first 30 seconds
- Include cards and end screens
- Optimize titles (60 characters max)
- Write detailed descriptions with keywords
- Always include clear disclosures for sponsored content

**For Shorts:**
- Repurpose Reels/TikTok content
- Fast-paced, value-dense
- Text overlays for silent viewing
- Clear CTA in final seconds

### Health Content Labeling

YouTube may label health content with:
- Health information panels linking to authoritative sources
- Context labels for certain health topics

**To be considered a credible health source**, organizations can apply through YouTube's Health Features program (requires licensed healthcare professional oversight).

## Facebook/Meta Ads

### 2025 Policy Changes

Meta has implemented significant limitations affecting wellness brands:
- Restricted ad targeting for health-related topics
- Limited tracking and conversion optimization
- Strict rules on health claims
- Prohibition on "negative self-perception" messaging

### Ad Specifications

**Primary Text:** 125 characters visible (up to 1,000 total)
**Headline:** 25-40 characters optimal (max 255)
**Description:** 25-30 characters optimal
**Image:** 1080x1080px (1:1) or 1080x1350px (4:5)
**Video:** Up to 240 minutes (15-30 seconds recommended for ads)

### Health & Wellness Ad Restrictions

**Prohibited:**
- Before/after images implying dramatic results
- Content generating "negative self-perception"
- Unverified health claims
- Dramatic transformation promises
- Weight loss guarantees
- Medical claims without substantiation

**Required:**
- Clear disclosure of paid content
- Honest representation of results
- Disclaimers on testimonials

### Best Practices

- Focus on emotional benefits over physical promises
- Use lifestyle imagery over results imagery
- Lead with education, not promotion
- Test multiple creatives
- Use Lead Ads for lower friction
- Retarget website visitors with educational content

### Targeting Limitations

Many health-related targeting options have been removed. Alternatives:
- Interest-based (yoga, meditation, healthy eating)
- Lookalike audiences from email lists
- Website visitor retargeting
- Engagement-based audiences

## Email Marketing

### Legal Requirements

**CAN-SPAM (US):**
- Must include physical mailing address
- Clear sender identification
- Honest subject lines
- Functional unsubscribe within 10 business days
- Penalty: Up to $50,120 per violation

**GDPR (EU/UK):**
- Explicit opt-in consent required
- Clear purpose statement for data use
- Easy unsubscribe option
- Right to data deletion
- Penalty: Up to â‚¬20 million or 4% of annual turnover

**CASL (Canada):**
- Express consent required
- Clear identification
- Unsubscribe mechanism
- One of the strictest regulations globally

### Content Specifications

**Subject Line:**
- Length: 40-60 characters optimal
- Avoid ALL CAPS, excessive punctuation
- Personalization (first name) increases open rates
- A/B test regularly

**Preview Text:**
- Length: 40-100 characters
- Complements subject line
- Visible in inbox before opening

**Body:**
- 300-500 words for newsletters
- Can be longer for educational content
- Single CTA performs best (371% higher CTR)
- Mobile-optimized (60%+ open on mobile)

### Structure

1. **Hook** (first line - most important)
2. **Story/Value** (educational content)
3. **Connection to offering** (natural transition)
4. **CTA** (single, clear action)
5. **Footer** (unsubscribe, address, social links)

### Must Include

- Unsubscribe link (visible, functional)
- Physical mailing address
- Sender name (company or individual)
- Privacy policy link
- Clear subject line matching content

### Must Avoid

- Buying email lists (illegal under GDPR, damages deliverability)
- Misleading subject lines
- Hidden unsubscribe links
- Excessive sending frequency without permission
- Health claims that violate platform policies
- ALL CAPS (triggers spam filters)
- Excessive exclamation marks!!!

### Best Practices

- Clean list regularly (remove inactive subscribers)
- Segment by interest/behavior
- Personalize beyond first name (past programs, interests)
- Send from recognizable sender
- Test send times for your audience
- Monitor deliverability metrics
- Re-engage inactive subscribers before removing

## Blog/Website

### SEO Requirements

**Title Tag:**
- 50-60 characters
- Primary keyword near beginning
- Compelling/clickable

**Meta Description:**
- 150-160 characters
- Include primary keyword
- Clear value proposition
- CTA if space allows

**URL Structure:**
- Short, descriptive
- Include primary keyword
- Use hyphens between words
- Avoid numbers/dates when possible

### Content Structure

**Article Length:**
- Minimum: 1,000 words for SEO value
- Optimal: 1,500-2,500 words
- Pillar content: 3,000+ words

**Heading Structure:**
- H1: One per page (title)
- H2: Main sections
- H3: Subsections
- Use keywords naturally in headings

**Internal Linking:**
- Link to related content (3-5 per article)
- Use descriptive anchor text
- Link to program pages where relevant

### Formatting

- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences)
- Bullet points and lists for scanability
- Images every 300-400 words
- Pull quotes for key insights
- Bold key phrases (don't overdo)

### Medical Compliance

- Include disclaimers on health-related content
- Link to professional sources when citing research
- Recommend consulting healthcare professionals
- Avoid definitive cure/treatment language

## Crisis Response Protocol

### Negative Comments/Reviews

1. **Acknowledge quickly** (within 4 hours)
2. **Don't delete** (unless clearly spam/hate)
3. **Move to private conversation** ("Let's discuss this privately - please DM us")
4. **Take responsibility** where appropriate
5. **Offer solution** (refund, additional support, etc.)
6. **Follow up publicly** ("Glad we could resolve this")

### Misinformation

If TheLifeCo content is flagged or removed:
1. Review platform's specific policy
2. Adjust language to comply
3. Appeal if flagging was incorrect
4. Document patterns for future prevention
