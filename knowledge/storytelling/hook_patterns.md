# TheLifeCo Hook Patterns & Templates

Proven hook patterns adapted from engagement psychology principles, tailored for TheLifeCo wellness content.

---

## The Psychology Behind Effective Hooks

### Why People Stop Scrolling

The brain pays attention to two things: **threats and opportunities**. You have roughly **0.5 to 1.5 seconds** to interrupt the scroll. Three mechanisms trigger this interruption:

1. **Pattern Interruption** - Violating expectations with something unexpected
2. **Curiosity Gap** - Creating information asymmetry the brain needs to resolve
3. **Loss Aversion** - Suggesting that not watching means continued problems

### The Dopamine Factor

Dopamine is an **anticipation chemical**, not a pleasure chemical. Every promise triggers dopamine release. Delay the payoff while maintaining credibility = sustained engagement. This is why open loops work.

## Hook Anatomy

Every effective hook has three components within **5-7 seconds**:

| Component | Function | Timing |
|-----------|----------|--------|
| **The Interrupt** | Stops the scroll | Immediate (0-1 sec) |
| **The Gap** | Creates curiosity | Within 3 seconds |
| **The Stake** | Makes them care | Within 5-7 seconds |

## Pattern 1: Mystery Hooks

**Psychology**: Present an outcome without explaining how it happened. The method becomes the mystery.

### Structure
> "[Remarkable outcome] using [unexpected/secret method]"

### TheLifeCo Examples

**Detox & Cleansing:**
- "I lost 7 kg in 14 days without exercising once — using a method doctors in Turkey have known for decades."
- "The fasting protocol that reversed my chronic inflammation in just one week."
- "I stopped taking all my medications after discovering what TheLifeCo doctors call 'autophagy activation.'"

**Mental Wellness:**
- "I cured my 10-year insomnia without pills — using a technique that takes 5 minutes before bed."
- "The reason I stopped having panic attacks has nothing to do with meditation."
- "What happened when I finally understood why I couldn't stop stress eating."

**Weight Management:**
- "I dropped 3 dress sizes without counting a single calorie — here's the plant-based secret."
- "The counterintuitive reason eating MORE helped me finally lose weight."
- "Why my nutritionist told me to stop dieting — and what I did instead."

**Longevity:**
- "The 2,000-year-old Turkish practice that's adding years to my life — proven by modern science."
- "What billionaires spend $100K on that you can access for a fraction of the cost."
- "The cellular reset that turned my biological age back 8 years."

## Pattern 2: Tension Hooks

**Psychology**: Create conflict or contradiction that demands resolution. Challenge what they believe to be true.

### Structure
> "Everything you've been told about [topic] is [wrong/harmful/backwards]"

### TheLifeCo Examples

**Detox & Cleansing:**
- "Everything you've been told about breakfast is not just wrong — it's actively sabotaging your metabolism."
- "The 'healthy' foods your doctor recommends are making your gut worse."
- "What if the supplements you're taking are actually preventing your body from healing?"

**Mental Wellness:**
- "The self-care advice everyone gives is making your burnout worse."
- "Why positive thinking is the worst thing you can do when you're stressed."
- "The meditation apps are lying to you — and here's why they don't work long-term."

**Weight Management:**
- "Exercise is keeping you fat. Here's the science they don't want you to know."
- "The reason calorie counting has never worked for you isn't willpower."
- "What happens when you stop trying to lose weight is the opposite of what you'd expect."

**Lifestyle Medicine:**
- "Your chronic fatigue isn't about sleep — and no amount of rest will fix it."
- "The real reason your blood sugar won't stabilize has nothing to do with sugar."
- "Why taking medication for your condition might be preventing you from healing."

## Pattern 3: Incomplete Story Hooks

**Psychology**: Begin a narrative and leave it suspended. The brain needs to know how it ends.

### Structure
> "[Time marker] [unexpected source] told me something that changed [outcome]"

### TheLifeCo Examples

**Guest Transformations:**
- "Last month, a guest arrived barely able to walk. What happened on day 7 changed everything."
- "A CEO came to us after three heart attacks. What he discovered surprised even our doctors."
- "She was told she'd need medication for life. Then she spent 3 weeks with us."

**Expert Insights:**
- "Our head doctor just showed me the research that changes everything we know about aging."
- "What a Tibetan monk taught me about stress that 20 years of therapy couldn't."
- "Last week, a Harvard researcher visited TheLifeCo. Here's what shocked him."

**Personal Discovery:**
- "I never understood why I was always tired until I saw my blood work after 7 days of detox."
- "Something shifted on day 4 of my water fast that I wasn't prepared for."
- "The conversation I had with myself on day 3 changed how I live my life."

## Pattern 4: Countdown Hooks

**Psychology**: Promise a specific number of items. The brain tracks whether it received all items.

### Structure
> "There are [number] things [causing problem/that work], and [specific teaser about one]"

### TheLifeCo Examples

**Problem-Focused:**
- "There are 5 things destroying your gut health without you realizing — and the third one is in your kitchen right now."
- "3 habits are aging you faster than smoking — and you probably do all of them daily."
- "The 4 foods keeping you inflamed, tired, and overweight — one of them is considered 'health food.'"

**Solution-Focused:**
- "3 morning rituals that will transform your energy — and the last one takes 30 seconds."
- "The 5 therapies that actually reverse aging — number 4 is what celebrities pay thousands for."
- "7 signs your body is begging for a detox — if you have 3 or more, watch this."

**Transformation-Focused:**
- "5 things that happen to your body in the first 72 hours of water fasting."
- "The 4 stages of detox — and why stage 3 is when the magic happens."
- "3 changes you'll notice within the first week of plant-based eating."

**Stress & Mental Health:**

## Pattern 7: Statistics Hooks

**Modern Life Statistics:**

## Pattern 8: Contrast Hooks

**Psychology**: The brain evaluates things relative to adjacent options. Position your solution against the inferior alternative.

### Structure
> "Most people do [common approach]. Here's what actually works."

### TheLifeCo Examples

**Diet & Nutrition:**
- "Most people cut calories to lose weight. Here's why cutting toxins works better."
- "Everyone talks about what to eat. Almost no one talks about when — and it matters more."
- "Diets focus on restriction. Real transformation starts with nourishment."

**Wellness Approach:**
- "Spas help you relax. We help you reset. There's a fundamental difference."
- "Doctors treat symptoms. We address root causes. Here's what that actually means."
- "Most wellness retreats are vacations. This is transformation. Here's the difference."

**Self-Care:**
- "Everyone says 'practice self-care.' Almost nobody teaches you how to actually do it."
- "Meditation apps give you 10 minutes of calm. We give you tools that last a lifetime."
- "You've tried supplements, diets, and apps. Here's why none of them worked."

## Open Loop Structures

### Stacking Loops for Retention

Operate multiple loops at different timescales:

| Loop Level | Scope | Example |
|------------|-------|---------|
| **Macro Loop** | Entire piece | "By the end, you'll know exactly why your body isn't healing" |
| **Medium Loop** | Sections | "That's the first problem — but the second one is actually more damaging" |
| **Micro Loop** | Sentence to sentence | "Which brings us to the part nobody talks about" |

### Progress Indicator Phrases

Keep viewers engaged by signaling payoff is coming:

- "Which brings us to the part that actually matters"
- "But here's where it gets interesting"
- "Now that you understand that, here's what most people miss"
- "This is the part that changed everything for me"
- "Stay with me — this is where the transformation happens"

### Transition Bridges

Never let a section end without opening a new loop:

- **Summary-and-Tease:** "So that's how your gut gets damaged — but here's the problem most people run into when they try to fix it."
- **Progress Marker:** "That's the first pillar. But the second one is actually more important."
- **Value Reminder:** "Now that you understand the science, you're ready for the part that actually makes this work."

## Text Overlay Guidelines

### First-Frame Text (Maximum Interrupt)

**Single Words:**
- "Stop."
- "Wait."
- "Wrong."
- "Truth."

**Power Phrases:**
- "What they don't tell you"
- "The real reason"
- "This changed everything"
- "Nobody talks about this"

### Effective Text Patterns

**Contradiction Text:**
- You say: "I stopped exercising and got healthier"
- Text shows: "STOPPED exercising" → "healthier than ever"

**Outcome Text:**
- Numbers, statistics, timeframes rendered as text
- "7 days" appearing while discussing transformation
- "87% improvement" while discussing results

**Question Text:**
- You say: "Let me show you what actually happened"
- Text reads: "What ACTUALLY happened?"

## Platform-Specific Recommendations

### Instagram Reels / TikTok
- **Best hooks:** Transformation, Tension, Statistics
- **Open with:** Visual contrast or surprising statement
- **Text:** Essential for muted viewing
- **Length:** Front-load value, 30-60 seconds optimal

### YouTube
- **Best hooks:** Mystery, Incomplete Story, Countdown
- **Can use:** Longer setups (but still hook in first 5 sec)
- **Text:** Optional but helpful for emphasis
- **Length:** Can be longer form with multiple loops

### Email Subject Lines
- **Best hooks:** Question, Statistics, Tension
- **Keep:** Under 50 characters
- **Avoid:** All caps, excessive punctuation

### LinkedIn
- **Best hooks:** Statistics, Contrast, Question
- **Tone:** More professional, credibility-focused
- **Open with:** Data or counterintuitive insight

## Hooks to AVOID

### Generic Promises
- "I'm going to show you how to be healthy" (no specific gap)
- "Watch this to transform your life" (vague, overused)
- "The best wellness tips" (not specific, no curiosity)

### Clickbait That Can't Deliver
- "The ONE thing that cures everything" (impossible to deliver)
- "Doctors HATE this trick" (cliché, damages credibility)
- "You won't BELIEVE what happened" (overused, skepticism trigger)

### Weak Openings
- Starting with background or context
- "Hi, I'm [name] and today we're going to talk about..."
- Asking permission: "Can I share something with you?"

### Medical Overclaims
- "This cures diabetes" (use: "supports blood sugar management")
- "Guaranteed results" (use: "evidence-based approach")
- "Miracle healing" (use: "transformation" or "reset")

## Hook Testing Framework

### Before Publishing, Verify:

- [ ] Pattern interrupt in first 1 second
- [ ] Curiosity gap established by second 3
- [ ] Personal stakes clear by second 5-7
- [ ] Text overlay reinforcing the hook
- [ ] Content actually delivers on the promise
- [ ] No medical overclaims
- [ ] Aligned with TheLifeCo brand voice (warm, educational, human)

### Measure Success By:

1. **Watch time percentage** > raw views
2. **Retention graphs** - identify drop-off points
3. **Engagement rate** - comments, shares, saves
